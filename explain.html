SFS IB MYP

Introduction

The IB MYP curriculum is a challenging curriculum that prepares the students for the IB DP course that students will be taking from Junior to senior years of highschool. The curriculum is designed to allow the student to expand his/her abilities to their best, and it pushes the students to try their best.


Grading System

To convert to GPA

  • 7=4.3
  • 6=4.0
  • 5=3.7
  • 4=3.0
  • 3=2.5
  • 2=2.0
  • 1=1.8
  • 0=0

4 is the passing grade for all subjects, and if prepared properly, there will be no problem of receiving a grade above or equal to 5. All students should take 7 subjects that are graded in total, and two other classes being Christian education and seminar.

Subjects

The 7 subjects are math, english lang&lit, design, science, 2nd language of choice, physical education, and history. Math courses are separated into three levels. HL, SL, and SL plus. HL courses cover all pre calc, and some derivatives and integrations during sophomore year. SL and SL plus students take the same courses, but SL plus students have one more class. The class covers only until pre calc until 10th grade. SFS MYP provides numerous different 2nd language courses, starting from Korean, French, Spanish, and more. The student can take the language of their own choice. Science courses change depending on the year. In 9th grade, all students are required to take biology, and in 10th grade, all students are required to take both chemistry and physics.

Test Types

The test types vary for each different criterions for all subjects. To explain, for the main subjects. (The maximum score one could get is 8)

Mathematics:

  • Crit A:In class assessment, only assessing the answer.
  • CritB&C: Discovering patterns and explaining the process comprehensively.
  • CritD:Real life application of mathematics

English:

  • Crit A:Sophisticated use of structure
  • CritB:Sophisticated use of diction
  • Crit C:Comfortably presenting the work
  • Crit D:Use of correct grammar and syntax

Design:

  • Crit A: Preliminary research(Client research-what they want). Identify the problem.
  • Crit B: Developing design specifications and structuring a model for the final design.
  • Crit C: Creating the actual final product. Needs a process journal.
  • Crit D: Evaluating the final product.
